# Section 1 — Source-by-Source Relevance Audit

*Pass 2 analytical core for the marketplace selling-copilot. Product context: a self-improving selling copilot for P2P resale (Depop / Vinted / Poshmark / eBay Best-Offer + a platform-agnostic core), built for casual sellers. Each book below is audited for what it contributes to **product behavior**, not for a book report. Stable IDs (PRIN-xxx, INTENT-xxx, OBJ-xxx, CONC-xxx, COACH-xxx) cross-link to the five canonical assets.*

The seven books do not overlap as much as their titles suggest. Read together they sort cleanly into four jobs the copilot has to do: **win the price** (claim value in a haggle), **build the deal** (expand beyond price), **set up the sale** (listing/audience/timing), and **keep it human and safe** (de-escalation, trust, ethics). The audit below tags each book to its job and to the ten product stages.

---

## 1. Never Split the Difference — Chris Voss

**Thesis.** Negotiation is emotional and System-1 driven, not rational problem-solving. You win by *tactical empathy* — vocalizing the other side's emotions so they feel understood and in control — then steering with active-listening tools (mirroring, labeling, calibrated How/What questions, silence), seeking "No" and "That's right" over "Yes," and anchoring + conceding on a disciplined ladder (Ackerman). "No deal is better than a bad deal."

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** This is the **single best source for ready-to-send message copy.** The mechanics map 1:1 onto turn-based, faceless marketplace chat: mirroring and labeling (PRIN-012, PRIN-013), the accusation audit (PRIN-014), the calibrated "How am I supposed to do that?" as the default lowball reply (PRIN-002), the "No"-oriented anti-ghosting DM (PRIN-015), letting the buyer anchor first (PRIN-001), odd/precise final numbers (PRIN-017), the Ackerman shrinking-increment ladder (PRIN-007), "don't split the difference" (PRIN-008), and the three-voice tone model (warm default + "DJ voice" for the firm line).

**Ignore-list (deliberately dropped).** Hostage/crisis/kidnapping operatics; enterprise/B2B/M&A/salary negotiation (wrong stakes and relationship model); in-person body-language reading (7-38-55, "Pinocchio nose") — text-only chat can't use it; negative leverage / real threats (toxic, and Voss himself warns against it); deep Black-Swan worldview excavation (too high-effort for a $30 sale).

**Applicability: 5/5.** Highest-value book for the reply generator. Its conversational unit (short, emotional, can't-see-the-other-person) *is* the marketplace DM. The only caution is gating its heavier rituals (Ackerman, Rule of Three) by item value.

**Single best product use.** The default reply engine for live haggling and objection chat — especially the lowball handler (PRIN-002) and the ghost-revival nudge (PRIN-015).

## 2. Getting to Yes — Fisher, Ury & Patton

**Thesis.** Positional bargaining (stake a position, defend, concede) is inefficient and damages relationships. Principled negotiation is *hard on the merits, soft on the people*: separate people from problem, focus on interests not positions, invent options for mutual gain, and insist on objective criteria. Power comes from your BATNA; judge every offer against it, not an arbitrary bottom line.

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** This is the **decision-logic and ethics spine**, not the phrasebook. Decode the interest behind the lowball (PRIN-004); justify price with objective criteria / sold comps (PRIN-003); "What's your theory?" before reacting to a number; invent options / dovetail differences (PRIN-005, PRIN-006); BATNA → reservation price and the **trip wire** instead of a rigid bottom line (PRIN-010); negotiation jujitsu and "trust is a separate issue" (feeds OBJ-rude-aggressive, OBJ-off-platform-request); reasoning-before-the-number close (PRIN-018).

**Ignore-list.** International diplomacy / arms-control examples (extracted for the mechanic only); the formal One-Text mediator procedure (assumes a neutral third party + many rounds); brainstorming-workshop logistics; written-contract / constituency / boss-approval machinery; no buyer-typing model is offered (deliberately).

**Applicability: 4/5.** The judgment layer that keeps the copilot fair and un-manipulative, but light on copy and tuned for longer, higher-stakes negotiations — so for a $25 Depop sale it must be applied selectively.

**Single best product use.** The pricing/accept-decline brain: comp-based price justification (PRIN-003) plus the BATNA trip wire (PRIN-010, COACH-001) that governs auto-decline thresholds.

## 3. Getting Past No — William Ury

**Thesis.** Difficult negotiations stall on five barriers (your reaction, their emotion, their position, their dissatisfaction, their power). You can't bulldoze them (the power paradox: the harder you make it to say no, the harder to say yes). The breakthrough is *indirect*: go to the balcony (don't react), step to their side, reframe, build a golden bridge, use power to educate not threaten.

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** The **de-escalation and reframing engine** for hostile, lowballing, and stonewalling buyers. Go to the balcony / cool-down (PRIN-019); step to their side & acknowledge first (PRIN-020); "Yes...and," never "but" + I-statements (PRIN-021); reframe a position into a shared problem; the golden bridge / face-saving cover (PRIN-022, CONC-007); warn-don't-threaten with your BATNA while keeping the offer on the table (PRIN-023); be generous at the end (PRIN-024); "prepare the prize" with three target prices.

**Ignore-list.** Hostage/terrorist and labor-strike/treaty cases; enterprise M&A (kept the golden-bridge principle, dropped the deal mechanics); in-person body-language tactics; bring-a-partner / caucus / physical time-out prep; third-party coalition/mediator moves (no natural third party in a 1:1 DM).

**Applicability: 4/5.** The best source for *tone control under conflict* and for turning a "$20 take it or leave it" into a workable thread; compressed from multi-session disputes to single async threads.

**Single best product use.** The hostile-thread handler: the balcony cool-down (COACH-008) + golden-bridge concession-with-cover (CONC-007) that lets a buyer climb off a public lowball.

## 4. Difficult Conversations — Stone, Patton & Heen

**Thesis.** Every hard conversation is three: "What Happened?" (truth/intent/blame), Feelings, and Identity. People err by assuming they're right, inventing the other's intentions, assigning blame, and mishandling feelings. The fix is a *learning stance*: separate impact from intent, map contribution not blame, acknowledge feelings before problem-solving, open from a neutral Third Story, and accept you can't control the other person's reaction.

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** The **emotional-intelligence and post-sale / safety layer.** Acknowledge feelings before problem-solving (PRIN-020, COACH-015); disentangle intent from impact when accused of scamming (PRIN-041, COACH-016); blame → contribution and own your real fault early (PRIN-042, COACH-017); the And Stance (hold price + validate feeling); open from the Third Story for ghosting/unpaid nudges; "I feel" not "you are" for off-platform refusals (PRIN-044); hit pause / question your attribution on terse messages (INTENT-014); name the dynamic + clarify consequences for review-extortion; "don't vent / don't hit-and-run" (COACH-010); you can't control their reaction (COACH-018).

**Ignore-list.** Deep relational contexts (marriage, parenting, grief, firing, trauma); in-person/phone channel guidance (kept only text-specific rules); identity-grounding as personal growth; truth/relativism and political-polarization philosophy; organizational-culture material.

**Applicability: 4/5.** Indispensable for the *complaint/dispute* arc and the anti-manipulation posture, but weak for active price-winning. Notably the lowest-risk book — its danger is the opposite of Influence's.

**Single best product use.** The post-sale dispute resolver (INTENT-013, OBJ-condition-doubt, CONC-011): acknowledge → own contribution → two fair options.

## 5. Influence — Robert Cialdini

**Thesis.** In an information-saturated world people rely on shortcuts ("click, whirr"): reciprocation, commitment & consistency, social proof, liking, authority, scarcity. Each is usually a sensible rule of thumb; compliance pros exploit them by manufacturing the trigger without the reality. The ethical line: the principles work *as well honestly as dishonestly*, so a seller never needs to fake them.

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** The **listing-copy and trust-framing source** — and the highest guardrail-risk book. Genuine social proof (PRIN-029); authority via real proof of authenticity (PRIN-030); liking via similarity & sincere compliments (PRIN-031); reciprocity / the small genuine gift and rejection-then-retreat (PRIN-024, PRIN-028); commitment & consistency / small-yes ladder + get-it-in-writing (PRIN-032, PRIN-025); scarcity & loss framing (PRIN-011); perceptual contrast / real-comp anchoring (PRIN-009); and critically, **reactance** → restore the buyer's freedom as the default tone (PRIN-033, COACH-014).

**Ignore-list.** Milgram obedience; cults/sects/Tupperware group dynamics (kept only as abuse exemplars); political logrolling & courtroom bias; boiler-room fraud; in-person-only mechanics (door-to-door BUG drop, retail floor staging); child-development reactance theory.

**Applicability: 5/5.** Maps almost 1:1 onto levers a reseller already pulls (saves, likes, reviews, scarcity, freebies). But *every* play has a manipulative twin — this book drives more guardrail flags (COACH-013) than any other.

**Single best product use.** The listing generator + trust copy (genuine social proof + authority), paired with the freedom-restorer (PRIN-033) as the system's default non-pushy tone.

## 6. Bargaining for Advantage — G. Richard Shell

**Thesis.** There's no single all-purpose strategy; start from self-knowledge (your bargaining style) and read each situation against six foundations (styles, goals/expectations, standards, relationships, the other's interests, leverage), then move through four stages (prep, info exchange, opening & concessions, closing). Two findings dominate: people who set specific, optimistic, justifiable goals get more; and leverage is psychological — it flows to whoever has the least to lose.

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** The **reasoning engine and buyer/seller classifier.** The five bargaining styles → the buyer-intent classifier (PRIN-026; INTENT-002/006/011, etc.); goals-not-bottom-line / high-but-defensible anchor (PRIN-009, COACH-002); leverage = balance of needs and fears, mapped to likes/watchers/days-on-market (PRIN-010); normative leverage / consistency principle (PRIN-003); probe-first-disclose-second (PRIN-001, COACH-005); shrinking ladder + If/then trades (PRIN-007, PRIN-005); reciprocity dance (PRIN-028); the Situational Matrix → match firmness to relationship vs. stakes (PRIN-027); close for *commitment* not agreement (PRIN-025); tell-the-truth-slowly (COACH-013); bargaining bullies (PRIN-040); **coach the seller around their own style's blind spot** (PRIN-045, COACH-020).

**Ignore-list.** Hostage/crisis as a leverage teaching device; M&A/JV/trade-deal machinery; labor/union coalition-building; cross-cultural/gender ritual specifics; in-person/channel-choice tactics; intra-org "Art of Woo"; the law-of-fraud appendix.

**Applicability: 4/5.** The richest source for the *classifier* and *seller coaching*, and the cleanest framework for "how firm should I be?" Weak on ready copy (that's Voss) and heavy on billion-dollar examples.

**Single best product use.** The buyer-style classifier (PRIN-026) that routes which reply template fires, plus style-aware seller coaching (PRIN-045/COACH-020).

## 7. 3-D Negotiation — Lax & Sebenius

**Thesis.** Most negotiators are "1-D" — they think it's only tactics at the table. You must play three dimensions concurrently: (1) tactics at the table, (2) deal *design* on the drawing board (structure value via differences — bundles, contingencies, risk/time allocation), and (3) *setup* away from the table (right parties, interests, no-deal options, sequence, process). Most of the game is won before anyone haggles. "Let them have your way."

**Most relevant concepts for this product.** The **product-architecture and listing-quality lens.** The 3-D barriers audit before dropping price (PRIN-034, COACH-006); map the parties → route to the highest-value buyer (PRIN-035); probe behind the position to the interest (PRIN-004); dovetail differences / hold-price-add-value (PRIN-005); move northeast / bundles (PRIN-006); anchor with comps + the justified "because" (PRIN-009); improve your no-deal option and worsen theirs with truthful scarcity (PRIN-010, PRIN-011); run buyers in parallel, not series (COACH-022); contingent terms & risk allocation to bridge doubt (PRIN-036); negotiate the spirit of the deal in the listing (PRIN-037, COACH-023); time-based dovetailing / markdown cadence (PRIN-038, COACH-021); meta-anchor the frame (PRIN-035); converging-concession ladder (PRIN-007).

**Ignore-list.** High-diplomacy/statecraft cases; large M&A / VC financing mechanics; enterprise coalition-building & corporate governance; labor strikes; formal mediation/ADR; principal-agent/broker problems; cross-cultural protocol. (Underlying principles kept; institutional apparatus dropped.)

**Applicability: 4/5.** The book that pushes the copilot upstream — most P2P "price problems" are really *setup* problems (bad photos, wrong audience, no bundle lever). Least useful at the chat-reply layer; most useful for listing-builder and system-design features.

**Single best product use.** The "why isn't this selling?" diagnostic (PRIN-034) + listing-builder (framing, comps, spirit-of-deal, markdown cadence) that prevents haggles before they start.

## How the Seven Books Divide the Labor

The corpus self-organizes into a pipeline. **3-D Negotiation** works furthest upstream — it builds the listing, picks the audience, frames the item, and sets the markdown cadence so most haggles are pre-empted (the metrics/learning-loop owner). **Influence** dresses that listing in honest trust signals (social proof, authority, scarcity) and sets the system's default non-pushy tone via reactance. **Bargaining for Advantage** reads the incoming buyer — classifying style and situation to route the right strategy — and coaches the seller against their own blind spots. When a live haggle starts, **Never Split the Difference** supplies the actual words (mirror, label, calibrated questions, the concession ladder, odd numbers), with **Getting to Yes** supplying the rational guardrails underneath (comps as objective criteria, BATNA-based accept/decline). When the buyer turns hostile or stalls, **Getting Past No** takes over the tone (balcony, reframe, golden bridge, warn-don't-threaten). And when something goes wrong after the sale, **Difficult Conversations** runs the dispute (acknowledge feelings, own contribution, two fair options) and, with Shell, anchors the anti-manipulation ethics. The tension the corpus must reconcile is real but bounded: Voss says never split the difference, Fisher says a comp-backed split is fair, and Cialdini's persuasion levers each have a manipulative twin that the Difficult Conversations / Shell honesty posture must police. Those conflicts are reconciled in the principles asset (notably PRIN-007/PRIN-008) and expanded in Section 2C.
