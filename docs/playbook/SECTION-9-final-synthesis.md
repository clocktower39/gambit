## 9.a Core philosophy (the unifying stance across all 7 books)

Across Voss's tactical empathy, Fisher-Ury's principled fairness, Ury's golden bridges, Shell's situational self-knowledge, Lax-Sebenius's setup-over-tactics, Cialdini's honest persuasion, and Stone-Patton-Heen's learning stance, **one stance unifies all seven: win the deal by making the *other side* feel safe, understood, and fairly treated — and by building a strong situation *before* the haggle — rather than by out-arguing or manipulating them at the table.** The copilot's job is to help a reluctant casual seller *set up well* (right buyer, honest comp-anchored listing, known floor/BATNA), *understand the buyer* (decode the interest behind the number, label the emotion, read the intent), and *close fairly and firmly* (anchor on the goal, concede on a shrinking ladder to a real floor, grow the pie with non-price trades, end generously) — all while **refusing to fabricate, pressure, or manipulate**, because every book independently concludes that honest moves work as well as dishonest ones and that a buyer who feels cheated reneges, disputes, and never returns. Setup beats tactics; empathy beats argument; fairness beats winning; honesty beats cleverness.

## 9.b The Top-10 lists

### 10 negotiation rules the copilot lives by
1. **Probe before you disclose** — never name your lowest first; bounce the question back (PRIN-001, COACH-005).
2. **Decode the interest behind the number** before countering (PRIN-004, COACH-007).
3. **Justify price with real sold comps**, reasoning before the figure (PRIN-003, PRIN-018).
4. **Anchor on your goal, not your floor** (PRIN-009, COACH-002).
5. **Know your BATNA; no deal beats a bad deal** — judge every offer against relist/hold (PRIN-010, PRIN-008).
6. **Hold price, add value** — trade cheap-to-you / valuable-to-them terms before cutting (PRIN-005, PRIN-006).
7. **Concede on a shrinking, conditional ladder — never for free** (PRIN-007, CONC-003).
8. **Lead with empathy: label the feeling, step to their side, before talking price** (PRIN-013, PRIN-020).
9. **Close for commitment, not just agreement** — confirm in writing, on-platform, immediately (PRIN-025).
10. **Be generous at the very end** — give the last small crumb so the buyer wins the last round (PRIN-024).

### 10 buyer signals to watch
1. Opening offer as **% of ask** (instant <50% → lowballer, INTENT-002).
2. **Reply latency / silence** (24h+ → ghosting/staller, INTENT-005).
3. **Specific item questions / stated budget** (serious buyer, INTENT-001).
4. **Multi-item likes** in one buyer (bundle opportunity, INTENT-006).
5. **Repeat-buyer / follower / local** status (accommodate, INTENT-011, PRIN-027).
6. **Likes/saves/watchers** count (truthful scarcity + BATNA strength, PRIN-011, PRIN-029).
7. **Authenticity / "is this legit?" questions** (fear, not price — INTENT-012).
8. **"I've seen it cheaper"** comparison anchoring (INTENT-003, OBJ-competitor-cheaper).
9. **Hostility / ultimatums / review threats** (bully vs. blunt competer, INTENT-007/008).
10. **Off-platform / odd-payment requests** — hard safety stop (INTENT-009, PRIN-044).

### 10 seller mistakes to prevent
1. Negotiating with **no floor/BATNA** set (COACH-001).
2. **Anchoring on the floor** and relaxing the moment an offer clears it (COACH-002).
3. **Caving / over-accommodating** to avoid confrontation (COACH-003, PRIN-045).
4. **Reflexively splitting the difference** (COACH-004, PRIN-008).
5. **Disclosing the lowest price first / over-explaining** (COACH-005).
6. **Slashing price when the listing is the real problem** (COACH-006, PRIN-034).
7. **Reacting instantly** to a rude/ambiguous message (COACH-008, PRIN-019).
8. **Panic-discounting** instead of timing the markdown (COACH-021, PRIN-038).
9. **Jumping to a fix before acknowledging feelings** on a complaint (COACH-015).
10. **Faking scarcity / comps / authenticity** because "it works" (COACH-013).

### 10 highest-value tactics
1. **Calibrated/problem-solving question** to deflect a lowball ("how am I supposed to do $20?") (PRIN-002).
2. **Comp-anchored price justification** (PRIN-003).
3. **Bundle to grow the pie / move northeast** (PRIN-006, CONC-006).
4. **Hold price, absorb shipping** as the meet (PRIN-005, CONC-005).
5. **Shrinking-increment concession ladder** to a real floor (PRIN-007, CONC-003).
6. **Label the emotion** to defuse hesitation/frustration (PRIN-013).
7. **"No"-oriented question** to revive a ghosting buyer (PRIN-015, OBJ-stalling-ghosting).
8. **Golden bridge** — face-saving cover for a stuck buyer (PRIN-022, CONC-007).
9. **Contingent terms / risk allocation** (returns, authentication) to answer doubt without a price cut (PRIN-036).
10. **Close for commitment on-platform, then be generous** (PRIN-025 + PRIN-024).

### 10 guardrails (anti-manipulation)
1. **Only true scarcity / social proof / comps / authenticity** — fabrication hard-blocked (COACH-013, PRIN-029, PRIN-030).
2. **Off-platform / scam = hard stop**, never negotiated (PRIN-044, INTENT-009, CONC-012).
3. **Never below the comp-supported floor/BATNA** (PRIN-010, COACH-001).
4. **State scarcity once, then restore the buyer's freedom** — no repeat nudges (PRIN-033, COACH-014).
5. **Go to the balcony** — no instant counter-punch to hostility (PRIN-019, COACH-008/009).
6. **Don't weaponize "fair" or feign emotion** (NSD/GTY guardrails, PRIN-021).
7. **Acknowledge feelings genuinely — never hollow boilerplate** (COACH-015, DC guardrail).
8. **Own real fault; don't dodge it with "we both contributed"** (PRIN-042, COACH-017).
9. **Warn with BATNA, never threaten; keep the offer on the table** (PRIN-023, COACH-011).
10. **Human-in-the-loop** — copilot suggests, the seller approves every send (§5.4).

## 9.d The riskiest assumptions to test first
1. **That high-stakes/in-person/professional book tactics transfer to casual, low-dollar, async resale at all** — the foundational bet of the product (§8.c #1). Test via A/B on conversion, price-vs-ask, and dispute rate.
2. **That the synthesized concession defaults (CONC-xxx) are right** — every discount % is a guess, likely category-dependent (§8.c #2). Tune as live thresholds.
3. **That tactical-empathy/labeling moves (PRIN-012/013/016) land in tone-stripped async chat** rather than reading scripted or patronizing (§8.c #3). Test via seller-edit rate and conversion on label-bearing vs. plain replies.

*(Runner-up, and the one with the worst downside: that the off-book scam/off-platform detector (INTENT-009, synthesized) has acceptable precision/recall — §8.c #8. Lower probability of being wrong, but highest cost if it is.)*
